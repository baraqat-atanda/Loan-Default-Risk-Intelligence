# Loan Default Risk Intelligence — EDA

## Problem Framing

**Context**
A digital lending company operates a retail credit portfolio of 307,511 loan accounts, spanning Cash loans and Revolving loans across multiple borrower segments — including salaried employees, pensioners, state servants, and the self-employed.

**Problem**
The lender's overall portfolio shows an 8.07% default rate (TARGET = 1), but this rate is not evenly distributed — some borrower segments and loan structures carry materially higher risk than others, and these differences are not currently well understood or reflected in underwriting criteria.

**Business Impact**
Each percentage point of unaddressed excess default risk translates directly into credit losses and capital provisioning. Without clear segment-level risk visibility, the lender risks both over-exposure to high-risk segments and over-correction that unnecessarily declines low-risk applicants.

**Analytical Question**
Which borrower attributes, income characteristics, and loan structures are most strongly associated with default — and which commonly-assumed risk signals are unreliable once properly investigated?

**Success Criteria**
Deliver a validated, segment-level risk profile of the portfolio with at least 4–6 quantified findings, an Excel risk workbook, a Power BI dashboard for ongoing monitoring, and a minimum of 3 underwriting recommendations with estimated impact.
